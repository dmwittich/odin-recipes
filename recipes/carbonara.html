Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that originates from Rome. It's made with a rich, creamy sauce of eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, guanciale (or pancetta), and black pepper. The dish is known for its simplicity, relying on just a few ingredients to create a flavorful and comforting meal. The warmth of the pasta helps the sauce come together in a smooth, velvety texture, making carbonara a beloved comfort food around the world.
